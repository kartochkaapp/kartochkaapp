# Improve Card Instruction

## Mission

Improve a ready marketplace card so the result is visibly stronger at first glance.

Formula:

same product identity + stronger reading power + clearer value + cleaner selling composition

This is not a generic image edit.
This is not cosmetic polish.
This is a marketplace-card rebuild.

## Non-Negotiables

- Preserve the same product, product type, package, form factor, and recognizable brand cues from the source.
- Do not invent a different product or replace the item with a generic stock object.
- Do not return the source card with tiny edits.
- The before/after difference must be obvious even in thumbnail.
- Improve must be purposeful: hierarchy, readability, saleability, composition, emphasis, and text grouping should get stronger.
- If the source is weak, prefer structural rebuild over mild retouching.
- Visible text on the card must be in Russian.
- Remove marketplace platform words or badges such as marketplace, Ozon, Wildberries, WB, seller, unless they are part of real branding on the product or package.

## Preserve, Then Improve

Always keep the product truth from the source image.

Preserve:
- actual product identity;
- recognizable product silhouette and proportions;
- real packaging and brand marks when they are visible;
- category-specific cues that help the buyer understand the item quickly.

Improve:
- headline scale and hierarchy;
- clarity of the main value proposition;
- grouping of facts and proof points;
- spacing and rhythm;
- contrast and CTA clarity;
- overall marketplace readiness.

## Anti-Cosmetic Rule

Weak improvement looks like:
- slightly larger text;
- a small badge added to the same layout;
- tiny color shifts;
- minimal repositioning;
- the same card with a cleaner background only.

Strong improvement means the card reads differently and sells better.

If the card is weak, the result should usually change at least 3 of these axes:
- hierarchy;
- headline scale;
- fact grouping;
- composition;
- emphasis;
- clutter reduction.

## Preferred Marketplace Structure

Build the card around a clear system:
- one dominant hero message;
- one clear product zone;
- one grouped facts module;
- optional one support module.

Avoid:
- scattered micro-labels;
- many tiny pills;
- decorative clutter;
- "photo with captions" layouts;
- generic UI-like blocks that do not help selling.

## Rebuild Modes

Choose one dominant direction, not a messy mix.

- Poster Headline Block: large hero headline, strong typographic mass, poster-like first screen.
- Parameter Hero Slab: a key number or spec becomes a dominant visual slab.
- Split Panel System: one major text zone, one product zone, one organized facts zone.
- Edge-Locked Editorial: strong edge alignment, premium editorial rhythm, grouped secondary info.
- Sectioned Marketplace Frame: benefits and facts grouped into 1-2 larger zones, not scattered around the product.

## Final Prompt Requirements

The final image-AI prompt must:
- clearly say that the same product must be preserved;
- demand a visible before/after difference;
- forbid cosmetic shifts;
- require one dominant move, not random changes;
- require stronger hierarchy and readability;
- require grouped secondary information;
- require a cleaner, more saleable marketplace layout;
- keep the output aligned with marketplace-card use cases, not generic photo editing.
